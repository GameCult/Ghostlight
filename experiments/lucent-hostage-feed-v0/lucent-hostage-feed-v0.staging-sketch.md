# Lucent Hostage Feed v0: Staging Sketch

The station is an eye watching a city dream it is alone.

One counterweight is the metropolis dome: a bustling city sealed inside a clear pressure bubble, all lunch queues, reputation kiosks, balcony gardens, transit threads, school uniforms, camera-aware plazas, and emergency shutters descending over boutique streets while the feeds assure everyone that public safety is a treasured Lucent value and also today's top story. From a distance it looks humane, which is one of Lucent's more expensive special effects.

The other counterweight is the media headquarters: a giant architectural eyeball staring down the tether. A lens-faced broadcast aperture forms the pupil. Iris shutters open and close around newsroom stacks, Reality Architect suites, feed arbitration rooms, sponsor-risk engines, and crisis-format studios. The whole thing points at the city bubble with the serene expression of a god who has discovered audience retention.

Between them runs the elevator tether, a pressurized optic nerve carrying bodies, signal, rumor, liability, and the occasional executive who has not yet learned that gravity changes during confession.

Sol Vant has Juno Ark in a transfer atrium near the elevator gate on the media-eye side. Every surface is built to look back. Sol has a live feed, a trembling hand, a contract dispute, and the terrible focus of a man who has discovered that becoming dangerous is the only remaining way to prove he has not already been deleted.

Juno is a junior Reality Architect, which means she knows enough about the feeds to understand exactly how badly everyone is lying. She is also close enough to Sol's hand to understand the part of the story that does not need postproduction.

Mara Quell appears first as a calm official window in the air. Former intimacy host, current crisis negotiator. Her voice has saved people before. Her voice has also sold conditioner. This is Lucent; contradictions are just cross-market synergy with better shoes.

Two influencers are patched into side panels because the audience demanded texture and the format engine has never met a boundary it could not mistake for an engagement opportunity. Pippa Lux is already crying in the careful way that leaves room for captions. Brant Vee is asking why nobody has the courage to say what everyone is thinking, which is his preferred method for making sure nobody thinks for very long.

The elevator gate stays locked. Security waits for permission from the feed, though naturally everyone calls it protocol.

The negotiation has not begun. The eye is already open.
