# Qwen Response: Scene 03 Maer/Veyr

Maer stands shoulder-to-shoulder with Veyr, his hand hovering near the terminal's power latch—not to strike, but to hold the line against a command that would sever the connection. The room is quiet, save for the hum of the cooling fans and the rhythmic thrum of the distress signal bleeding through the air.

"You speak of it as derivative firmware," Maer says, his voice low and steady, carrying the weight of a ritual he must not break. "But the ledger does not record derivatives. It records debts. It records lives."

He leans in slightly, forcing Veyr to turn from his polished screen to face him.

"Is the entity on that line a component of your chain, or a passenger who has been lost to it? Name the cost, Veyr. If you cannot name the cost of their silence, then your compliance vocabulary is merely a new form of abandonment. Do not wipe the terminal. The rescue window closes in seconds, not minutes. Speak to the person, not the product."
